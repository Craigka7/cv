My Career as a Healthcare Worker

I have been extremely blessed to work and learn at many different hospitals and clinics, each with a unique personality of its own (and equipped with its own set of challenges).

  • Weirton Medical Center
    • Working as a Phlebotomist at WMC gave me a ton of venipunture experience, along with a sense of what it was like to work with an elderly population

  • Triniy West Hospital
    • Trinity West afforded my my first experience in techwork, in a smaller and supportive atmosphere where I could focus on details and technique perfection. It also allowed me to experince the fast-paced environment of the ER, the behind-the-scenes of morning phlebotomy rounds, and rotations to night shift aquainted me with QC procedures

  • UPMC Childrens Hospital
    • Children's Hospital was a whole different animal! With hundreds of tests processed at breakneck speeds, precision, multitasking and time management were key.

  • MHS Laboratories
    • MHS Labs afforded me the insight of a startup company and the development thereof; it taught the process of Quality management and Policy writing

  • The Hillman Cancer Center
    • The Hillman Center is literally my dream job; I get to balance a busy lab environment with a small, close-knit staff and constant phlebotomy practice and patient interaction. I get to be immersed in the process from start to finish; from drawing the blood, to ordering and processing the test, to running the analysis, to the delivery of the results to the comupter system and/or the patient. I also am expected to maintain inventory, perform competency testing, and review QC monthly.

  • The Mabundene Healthcare Team in Africa
    • Working in an unofficial healthcare setting in Mozambique was lifechanging. Skills are tested at a new level when you must overcome a language barrier to teach said skills to other people who have never encountered the information before.
